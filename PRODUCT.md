# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, hiring managers, engineering leads, potential collaborators, and engineering peers evaluating Rivaldi Putra as a frontend and product engineer. They are likely scanning quickly on desktop or mobile to understand engineering taste, production experience, and whether Rivaldi can build practical interfaces for dashboards, internal tools, data-heavy products, and frontend systems.

## Product Purpose

This site presents Rivaldi Putra as a frontend/product engineer who builds clean, practical, production-ready interfaces. It should make visitors feel that Rivaldi cares about structure, clarity, craft, and real product execution, not only state those qualities in copy. Success means visitors confidently click through to the resume, email, LinkedIn profile, or selected projects and case studies.

## Brand Personality

Precise, craft-focused, grounded. The site should feel calm, intentional, readable, and engineered. It can show taste and visual personality, but it should remain practical, professional, and confident rather than loud or trend-driven.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS landing pages, overly corporate resume pages, flashy hacker or terminal-themed developer portfolios, AI-generated beige editorial templates, chaotic animation-heavy portfolios, and templates that look like every other developer site. The site should not rely on loud effects, vague tech branding, generic card grids, or decorative styling that does not support clarity.

## Design Principles

1. Make structure visible: hierarchy, spacing, and navigation should communicate the same clarity expected from production frontend work.
2. Let craft feel practical: refined details should support readability, scanning, and conversion rather than become decoration.
3. Prioritize evidence over claims: projects, tools, experience, and writing should demonstrate real product execution.
4. Stay grounded under polish: calm motion, restrained copy, and precise layout should make the portfolio feel trustworthy.
5. Design for quick evaluation: resume, email, LinkedIn, and project paths should be easy to find on both mobile and desktop.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Maintain strong mobile readability, clear contrast between text and background, keyboard-friendly navigation, and reduced-motion friendly animations. Avoid relying only on color to communicate state. Keep animation subtle, useful, and non-distracting.
